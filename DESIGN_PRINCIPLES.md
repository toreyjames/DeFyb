# DeFyb Design Principles

## 1. Clinic-First, Not Startup-First
Design should feel like a trusted practice workflow system, not a hype product.
Prioritize clarity, legibility, and operational calm.

## 2. Revenue Workflow Over Feature Noise
The primary journey is encounter review -> coding recommendation -> finalize.
Every screen should make that path faster.

## 3. Familiar EHR-Like Patterns
Use clear cards, stable spacing, and straightforward labels.
Avoid flashy transitions, novelty UI, and unnecessary visual effects.

## 4. Respectful Visual Tone
Typography, color, and iconography should feel professional and clinical.
No aggressive AI branding language in core workflow areas.

## 5. Signal Over Decoration
Highlight only what matters: suggested code, confidence, rationale, gaps, and revenue delta.
Secondary information should stay visually secondary.

## 6. Low Cognitive Load
Keep interactions short and predictable.
One clear primary action per step.

## 7. Consistency Across Public + Product UI
Landing, login, and tool views should share a coherent design language:
same typography, color hierarchy, and component behavior.

## 8. Trust Through Transparency
Recommendations must be explainable and auditable.
Visual design should reinforce confidence and accountability.
